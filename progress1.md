# Progress Report 1 – *Forsaken*

## Initial Goals and Feedback (5 pts)

Our initial goals for *Forsaken* were to establish the core player mechanics and begin shaping the game's atmosphere through UI and audio. Specifically, we aimed to implement basic character movement (walking, jumping, crouching), start building out the pause/menu system, and lay the groundwork for combat by designing scriptable weapon objects.

While we haven’t received formal instructor feedback yet, within the team we’ve been regularly reviewing each other’s work. A consistent piece of internal feedback was to maintain clarity between what is functional and what’s place-holder, so we’ve begun tracking in-progress vs. implemented features more clearly in our GitHub issues board.

## Summary of Progress:

We’ve made strong progress aligning with our initial goals:
- Implemented **basic movement mechanics** for the player character, including jumping and crouching.
- Designed a **pause menu UI** with working functionality, along with a sound effect when the player pauses the game.
- Created **scriptable weapon objects**, which will later support the player’s ability to switch weapons or use boss abilities.
- Met with our composer, **Phoenix**, who is now developing:
  - Multiple versions of the **main theme** to reflect the game’s emotional tone.
  - A **menu theme** for the title screen.
  - A variety of **sound effects** to layer into gameplay.
- Finalized **designs for the bosses**, which are aligned with the stages of grief. These designs will help guide future level layouts and influence the design of lower-tier enemies.

## Evaluation of Progress Since Proposal:

Overall, our progress matches our expectations. Basic movement was implemented smoothly, and the UI work came together quickly thanks to effective communication among team members. One area that still needs work is the **weapon mechanic system**—we’ve laid the foundation with scriptable objects, but haven’t hooked those into gameplay yet. We’re also beginning to consider how each boss and level will reflect specific emotional themes, which is helping us visualize the game’s arc more clearly.


## Outline plans to meet goals:

We’re actively coordinating through weekly meetings and daily check-ins on Whatsapp. Each team member has clear responsibilities, and we're using GitHub Projects to track task progress. As we shift into the next development phase, we’re focusing on connecting the design of our bosses and themes to the rest of the game, including enemy behavior, level layout, and combat systems. We're adjusting deadlines and sprint goals as needed based on the scope of new tasks.
